Here are some books and readings that have influenced my thinking and that I think are worth sharing.

Philosophy & Existentialism

"The Myth of Sisyphus"
by Albert Camus
Camus' exploration of the absurd and the human condition. His central question - whether life is worth living in the face of meaninglessness - resonates deeply with anyone who has questioned the purpose of their work or existence. Essential reading for understanding how to find meaning in seemingly futile endeavors.
"Thus Spoke Zarathustra"
by Friedrich Nietzsche
Nietzsche's masterpiece on the Übermensch and the will to power. Beyond the philosophical concepts, it's a meditation on creating your own values and finding strength in adversity. Particularly relevant for entrepreneurs and builders who must forge their own path.

Systems & Human Nature

"Antifragile"
by Nassim Nicholas Taleb
Taleb's concept that some systems actually benefit from disorder and stress is revolutionary. For founders and builders, understanding how to create antifragile systems - whether in business, technology, or personal life - is crucial for long-term success in an uncertain world.
"Sapiens"
by Yuval Noah Harari
A sweeping history of human civilization that reveals how our species' success lies in our ability to create and believe in shared fictions - from money to nations to corporations. Understanding these "imagined orders" is key to building products and companies that resonate with human psychology.
